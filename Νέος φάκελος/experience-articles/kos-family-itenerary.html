3 Perfect Days in Kos: A Family-Friendly Itinerary

Posted on July 28, 2025 by Ioannis

Kos is a fantastic destination for families, offering a wonderful mix of beautiful beaches, history, and fun activities. Planning a short trip can be daunting, so we've created this simple 3-day itinerary to help you make the most of your time without exhausting the little ones. It's all about balance!

Day 1: Beach Bliss and Town Exploration

Morning: Start your holiday the right way—at the beach! Lambi Beach is just a two-minute walk from our apartments. The water is calm and shallow, making it perfect for kids. Rent a pair of sunbeds and an umbrella, build some sandcastles, and enjoy a refreshing dip in the Aegean.

Afternoon: After a casual lunch at a beachside taverna, head back for a rest. In the late afternoon, take a leisurely stroll or cycle into Kos Town. The main promenade is flat and great for little legs. Explore the harbour, marvel at the huge yachts, and treat everyone to a delicious ice cream from a local gelateria.

Evening: Enjoy dinner in the charming Eleftherias Square, where the kids can run around a bit while you relax.

Day 2: History and Hot Springs

Morning: Introduce the kids to history in a fun way. Visit the Castle of Neratzia at the harbour. Kids love exploring the old walls and pretending to be knights. It doesn't take too long to see and offers great views. Afterwards, wander over to the Plane Tree of Hippocrates for a quick story about the father of medicine.

Afternoon: For a unique experience, drive or take a bus to the Therma Beach (about 15 minutes away). Here, natural hot springs flow into the sea, creating a natural, warm spa pool. Kids are fascinated by the warm water. Go in the late afternoon when the sun isn't as strong.

Day 3: Mountain Village and Sunset

Morning: Rent a car and drive up to the mountain village of Zia. The drive itself is beautiful. Zia is a picturesque village with stunning panoramic views of the island. Browse the little shops for local crafts and herbs. Enjoy a traditional lunch at one of the tavernas—the view is worth the trip alone.

Afternoon: On your way back, stop at Plaka Forest, a unique spot known for its resident peacocks and cats. The kids will be absolutely thrilled to see the peacocks roaming freely and showing off their feathers.

Evening: For your final evening, find a spot on the western coast near Mastichari or head back to Lambi to watch one of Kos's spectacular sunsets. It's the perfect, peaceful end to a wonderful family adventure.
