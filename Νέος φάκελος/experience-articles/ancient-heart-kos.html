Beyond the Beach: Discovering the Ancient Heart of Kos Town

Posted on July 20, 2025 by Ioannis

While the beaches of Kos are undeniably beautiful, the island's true soul lies in its incredible history. Kos Town is a living museum, where ancient ruins sit casually alongside modern cafes. Staying at Diamante Del Mar places you within a short, pleasant walk of some of the most significant archaeological sites in the Dodecanese. Here's a short guide to discovering the ancient heart of the city.

The Ancient Agora

Just a 10-minute walk from us lies the vast Ancient Agora. This was once the commercial and social center of ancient Kos. Today, it's a sprawling, atmospheric site where you can wander among the foundations of temples, houses, and columns. You can see parts of the old city wall, the sanctuary of Aphrodite, and a Christian basilica from a later period. It's a peaceful place to walk and imagine the vibrant marketplace it once was.

The Roman Odeon

A little further into town, you'll find the beautifully preserved Roman Odeon. Built in the 2nd century AD, this small theatre could seat around 750 spectators. The first nine rows of marble seats have been restored, and you can freely walk onto the stage and climb the seats. It's an incredibly evocative site, and it's easy to picture the musical contests and performances that once took place here. The acoustics are still impressive!

Casa Romana

Close to the Odeon is the Casa Romana, a stunningly restored Roman villa that provides a fascinating glimpse into the life of a wealthy Kos resident during the Roman period. The villa is built around three atriums, featuring beautiful mosaic floors, frescoes, and statues. It shows just how luxurious life was for the island's elite. The level of preservation and restoration makes it one of the most impressive historical sites in Kos.

The Asklepion (A Short Drive Away)

While not in Kos Town itself, no historical tour is complete without visiting the Asklepion, the most significant archaeological site on the island. Just a 10-minute drive, this was one of the most important healing centers of the ancient world, dedicated to the god of health, Asclepius. Hippocrates himself is said to have taught here. The site is built on three magnificent terraces on a hillside, offering breathtaking views. It's a place of profound peace and history that shouldn't be missed.
