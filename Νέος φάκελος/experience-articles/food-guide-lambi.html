A Food Lover's Guide to Lambi Beach: Our Top 5 Tavernas & Restaurants

Posted on August 2, 2025 by Ioannis

One of the greatest joys of visiting Kos is indulging in its incredible culinary scene. The Lambi area, right on our doorstep, is brimming with fantastic places to eat, from traditional, family-run tavernas to more modern beachfront restaurants. After many years of "dedicated research," here are our five personal favorites that we recommend to all our guests.

1. Taverna Avra (The Breeze)

Just a short stroll down the beachfront, Avra is the quintessential Greek taverna. The tables are practically on the sand, and the atmosphere is always lively and welcoming. The owner, Nikos, prides himself on serving the freshest fish, often caught the same morning. You must try their grilled octopus and the simple, perfect Greek salad. It’s not fancy, but it’s authentic and delicious.

2. The Olive Tree Kitchen

Tucked away on a side street, The Olive Tree is a true hidden gem. This is where you go for expertly cooked traditional "magirefta" (slow-cooked dishes). Their moussaka is legendary, and the lamb kleftiko, slow-cooked in parchment paper with herbs and cheese, is a dish you'll dream about long after you leave. The courtyard, shaded by an old olive tree, is perfect for a romantic dinner.

3. Blue Fin Seaside

For a more contemporary dining experience, Blue Fin is our top choice. It offers a sophisticated take on Greek seafood with a slightly more upscale vibe. The menu is creative, featuring dishes like sea bass ceviche and tuna tartare alongside perfectly grilled fish. It's a great spot for a special occasion, with stunning sunset views over the water.

4. Yia-Yia's Courtyard (Η Αυλή της Γιαγιάς)

This charming spot truly feels like you're eating in a Greek grandmother's backyard. The menu consists of classic, comforting dishes made with love and time-honored recipes. It's a no-frills establishment focused purely on flavour. Their keftedakia (meatballs) and gemista (stuffed tomatoes and peppers) are must-tries. It’s incredibly popular with locals, which is always a good sign.

5. Lambi Grill House

Sometimes, you just want a fantastic, no-fuss souvlaki. Lambi Grill House is the undisputed champion in our area. Whether you prefer pork, chicken, or a vegetarian option with halloumi, the skewers are always perfectly grilled, juicy, and flavourful. Grab a couple of pitas to go for a perfect beach lunch, or sit at their casual tables and enjoy the lively street scene. It's simple, affordable, and consistently excellent.
